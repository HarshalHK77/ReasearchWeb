Thermal Analysis of Lithium-Ion Battery Integration with Phase Change Material

Description

This study examines the integration of phase change materials (PCMs) to regulate the thermal behavior of lithium-ion batteries (LIBs). By absorbing and releasing heat during charge and discharge cycles, PCMs offer a promising solution to mitigate thermal runaway and enhance battery safety and longevity. The investigation employs experimental analysis, numerical simulations, and theoretical models to optimize PCM properties for effective battery temperature regulation. Results provide insights into enhancing the safety and reliability of lithium-ion battery systems.


thermal analysis of savonius wind turbine

Description

This study presents a thermal analysis of a Savonius wind turbine, exploring its performance under varying environmental conditions. The investigation employs computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations to analyze heat transfer mechanisms within the turbine structure. Key parameters such as material properties, ambient temperature, and wind speed are considered to assess the turbine's thermal behavior. Results indicate significant temperature variations across different components of the turbine, highlighting areas prone to thermal stress and potential efficiency improvements. The findings offer valuable insights for optimizing the design and operation of Savonius wind turbines, enhancing their performance and longevity in renewable energy applications.


Thermal analysis of steam generators

Description

This study presents a comprehensive thermal analysis of steam generators employing Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations. By leveraging advanced numerical techniques, the research investigates the intricate fluid dynamics and heat transfer phenomena within steam generators, crucial components of various industrial processes. Through CFD modeling, this study elucidates the complex interplay between fluid flow patterns, temperature distributions, and heat transfer efficiencies within steam generator systems. The findings contribute to optimizing the design and performance of steam generators, enhancing their energy efficiency, reliability, and operational safety in diverse engineering applications.

Thermal analysis of thermosyphons

Description

Thermosyphons are passive heat transfer devices widely employed in various engineering applications due to their simplicity and effectiveness. This study investigates the thermal performance of a thermosyphon using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations. The analysis focuses on understanding the heat transfer characteristics, fluid flow patterns, and temperature distribution within the thermosyphon under different operating conditions. Through CFD simulations, insights into the fluid behavior, convective heat transfer mechanisms, and thermal gradients are obtained, aiding in the optimization of thermosyphon design and performance enhancement. The findings provide valuable information for improving the efficiency and reliability of thermosyphon-based heat transfer systems across diverse industrial applications
